---
title: What is Attention Commons
description: An introduction to the Attention Commons project — what it is, why it exists, and the short version of the idea.
---

Attention Commons is a shared space for thinking about attention as a collective resource and developing the practices, governance models, and cultural forms needed to protect it.

## What it is

Attention Commons names two things at once: an argument and a proposal.

The argument is that attention — the capacity for awareness, presence, and agency that all conscious beings share — is being enclosed. Platforms, advertising, and algorithmic feeds extract attention as a commodity, treating what should be held in common as a private resource to be harvested. This is what researchers like James Williams and Matthew Crawford, and organisations like the Center for Humane Technology, have been documenting under the heading of the "attention economy."

The proposal is that a counter-tradition already exists — in contemplative practice, in teaching lineages, in sanghas and retreat centres — that holds the wisdom *about* attention as something to tend in common rather than sell. The task is to make that counter-tradition legible as a commons: to name it, to think about how it is governed, and to ask what it would take to scale it.

Attention Commons is also the commons-and-governance arm of a broader frame: **Culture of Attention (Культура внимания)** — a philosophical and practical project for cultivating attention in individuals, groups, communities, and institutions. Culture of Attention is the outward-facing movement; Attention Commons is its underlying philosophy and its civic-commons argument.

This is not a finished position. It is a live thinking-space — early and provisional.

## Why it matters

Two things are true at the same time. First, the range of practices that cultivate sustained, embodied attention — from basic mindfulness to deep contemplative training — is culturally and scientifically real. Second, nearly every contemplative form imported into the West has eventually been dissolved into wellness content, severed from the lineages and governance structures that held it.

The question Attention Commons is sitting with is whether there is a mode of collective stewardship that keeps practice transmissible, the lineages attributed, and the governing structures intact — without requiring withdrawal from the world. If practices cultivating deep, wide-aperture awareness produce agents who are structurally less capturable by distraction-and-extraction machines, then collective stewardship of attention has consequences that reach beyond personal wellness.

The four-quadrant strategic map developed by Viktor Shiryaev in 2026 identified the interesting territory as three "voids" in the current landscape: there is no common stewardship between the wellness market and isolated monasteries; no shared culture or language for attentiveness in families, teams, and cities; and the science of deep contemplative states is only beginning. Attention Commons is not another meditation app or mindfulness school — those zones are already crowded. It is oriented toward the gaps.

## The short version

The attention economy encloses attention like English landlords once enclosed common fields — redirecting a shared resource toward private extraction. A counter-tradition holds wisdom about attention as something to be tended in common, with attribution to lineages and teachers, governed like any other commons, and kept accessible. Attention Commons is an attempt to name that counter-tradition and think through what it would take to sustain it.

The concept sits inside *Culture of Attention* as its governance and philosophical arm. Neither is a finished thing.
