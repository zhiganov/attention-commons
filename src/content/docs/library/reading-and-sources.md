A curated reading list across the three main streams that inform Attention Commons: commons theory, contemplative practice, and futures and integral thinking.

These are not comprehensive bibliographies. They are the sources that have most directly shaped the thinking here, as gathered in the [development log](https://github.com/zhiganov/active-solidarity/blob/main/docs/development-log.md) and [research notes](https://github.com/zhiganov/active-solidarity/blob/main/research/README.md).

## Commons

- **Elinor Ostrom** — *Governing the Commons* (1990). The foundational empirical argument that commons governance is a viable third way beyond market and state. Her eight design principles for long-enduring commons are the structural reference for thinking about attention communities.

- **David Bollier** — *Think Like a Commoner* (2nd ed., released to open access); *Free, Fair and Alive* (with Silke Helfrich); *Patterns of Commoning*. Bollier's key contribution: commoning is a *social practice* and a process of becoming, not just a resource-management technique. A [Think Like a Commoner MCP server](https://github.com/zhiganov/think-like-a-commoner-mcp) makes the book conversationally accessible.

- **P2P Foundation** — [Four Future P2P Scenarios](https://wiki.p2pfoundation.net/Four_Future_P2P_Scenarios) and [Resilient Communities](https://wiki.p2pfoundation.net/Resilient_Communities) (Michel Bauwens and collaborators). Transnational "phyles" — alliances of ethical enterprises around a shared knowledge commons — as a governance reference. Wikipedia cited as one of the most successful global knowledge commons.

- **Pierre-Joseph Proudhon** — *What is Property?* (1840). Mutualism as a 19th-century precursor of commons thinking.

- **Peter Kropotkin** — *Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution* (1902). The evolutionary case for cooperation as a structural force.

## Contemplative

- **Ken McLeod / Unfettered Mind** — McLeod's entire body of Vajrayana teaching is available at [unfetteredmind.org](https://unfetteredmind.org/first-steps/) under a Creative Commons licence (attribute, don't alter, don't profit). A working example of a contemplative knowledge commons — practices held openly, with attribution, without enclosure.

- **Buddhist modernism** — [Wikipedia overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhist_modernism). The 19th–20th century movement that translated contemplative frameworks into secular and universalist terms; background for understanding how lineage transmission and adaptation interact.

- **Pure Land Buddhism** — [Wikipedia overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pure_Land_Buddhism). The Mahayana tradition oriented toward creating "Pure Lands" — environments in which the conditions for liberation are held collectively. A structural analogy for what "tending the conditions for attention" might mean at a community level.

- **Richard Davidson and Antoine Lutz** — pioneers of the neuroscience of advanced contemplative states. Flagged in the research notes as a field that exists but is not yet a discipline with reproducible protocols — the "individual exterior deep" void on Viktor Shiryaev's strategic map.

- **Francisco Varela, Evan Thompson, Eleanor Rosch** — *The Embodied Mind* (1991). The founding text of neurophenomenology; the meeting point of cognitive science and first-person contemplative inquiry.

## Futures & integral

- **Ken Wilber** — integral theory; the individual/collective × interior/exterior quadrants as a strategic map. The "everyone is right about something" heuristic as a working approach to lineage pluralism. The quadrant framework underlies the four-quadrant strategic map developed by Viktor Shiryaev, which identifies the underoccupied zones Attention Commons is pointed toward.

- **Rufus Pollock** — ["Scaling up solidarity, scaling down systems"](https://cultural-evolution-paradigmatic-change-rufuspollock.flowershow.me/cultural-evolution-paradigmatic-change.html): solidarity at planetary scale, and reduced systemic complexity (re-localised food, decentralised energy, stronger local interdependency) — a worldview that holds both global and local. This is where "active solidarity" now lives within the broader frame.

- **"We May Be Entering a Second Axial Age"** — [Noema](https://www.noemamag.com/we-may-be-entering-a-second-axial-age). Background reading on the hypothesis that civilizational-scale shifts in meaning-making are underway.

- **"Toward Wayfinding Infrastructure for the Living Web"** — [Atlas Research Group](https://www.atlasresear.ch/blog/toward-wayfinding-infrastructure-for-the-living-web/). On the conditions for navigating a complex information environment — adjacent to the attention commons problem.
