# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers who arrive to *understand*, not to operate a tool:

- **Contemplative practitioners and teachers** — people with a serious relationship to meditation and the contemplative traditions, who want the ideas treated with depth and accuracy, not as wellness content.
- **Commons- and governance-curious readers** — people interested in Ostrom, Bollier, P2P/commons thinking, and how those frames apply to attention.
- **The metacrisis / integral / "second renaissance" adjacent** — readers who think about coordination, attention, and civilizational questions.
- **Potential contributors** — a smaller set who may edit or add pages via GitHub.

Context of use: reading, often long-form, often unhurried, frequently on a phone or laptop in a quiet moment. They are giving the site their attention, and the site should honour that.

## Product Purpose

A public, contributable **knowledge commons about attention** — the argument that attention is a shared resource being enclosed by the attention economy, and the counter-tradition (contemplative, commons-theoretic, integral) that holds the wisdom about attending well as something to be tended in common. Success looks like: a credible, browsable, citable body of thinking that a newcomer can understand and a contributor can extend, sitting inside the broader *Culture of Attention* frame.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **contemplative, scholarly, provisional.** The voice is sober and unhurried, closer to a good essay or a university-press edition than to a product or a movement pitch. It has authority without hype, and it is explicit that nothing here is a finished position. It practises what it preaches: a site about attention must not fracture the reader's attention.

## Anti-references

- **Meditation/wellness apps** (Calm, Headspace) and the sage-green, soft-gradient, "breathe" zen-minimal look. This is the first reflex for the category; avoid it.
- **Wellness-SaaS / mindfulness-startup** landing pages: hero-metric blocks, identical feature-card grids, pastel gradients.
- **Default-Starlight / generic-docs** appearance, untouched and characterless.
- **Corporate thought-leadership** gloss and motivational-poster certainty.
- Anything that reads as AI-generated: no generated logos, illustrations, OG art, or AI-tool attribution.

## Design Principles

- **Reading is reverent.** Every choice serves sustained, comfortable reading. The medium is the message: a commons for attention cannot be attention-fracturing.
- **Provisional, not promotional.** Show thinking in progress. Stubs and open questions are honest, not embarrassing. No marketing voice.
- **Sources over assertions.** Claims are traceable to named references; the wiki is a commons of cited knowledge, not opinion.
- **Calm authority.** Contemplative depth and scholarly credibility, held quietly. Confidence without volume.
- **Built to be added to.** It should feel like something a reader could contribute to, not a finished monument.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. High text contrast and a generous reading measure (65–75ch). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (motion is minimal regardless). Don't rely on colour alone for meaning. Assume some readers are older or non-technical; legibility and plain navigation beat cleverness.
